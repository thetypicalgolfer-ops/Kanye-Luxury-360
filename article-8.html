Living in Stone Oak, San Antonio: The 2026 Insider's Guide for Luxury Homebuyers

Of all the neighborhoods carved into San Antonio's far north side over the past four decades, none has matured into the city's family-luxury anchor quite like Stone Oak. It is the rare master-planned community that has held its character through three full housing cycles — the boom of the early 2000s, the correction of 2008 to 2012, and the post-pandemic re-pricing of 2020 to 2024 — and emerged each time with stronger fundamentals than before. Today, Stone Oak is one of the most consistently demanded zip codes in San Antonio, and for buyers searching for the intersection of acreage-feel privacy, top-tier schools, and walkable luxury retail, there is genuinely no equivalent in the city.

This guide is written for buyers who are seriously evaluating Stone Oak — relocating professionals, executive families moving from coastal markets, San Antonio residents trading up from Alamo Heights or Hollywood Park, and the growing cohort of out-of-state buyers discovering the area for the first time. Below is a full picture of the geography, the sub-neighborhoods, the schools, the lifestyle, and the 2025–26 luxury market dynamics that should inform your decision.

What Stone Oak Actually Means

"Stone Oak" is used in three overlapping ways in San Antonio, and the distinction matters when you are searching listings or evaluating a neighborhood. The original Stone Oak refers to the master-planned community developed beginning in the late 1980s on the north side of Loop 1604, between US 281 and the Cibolo Creek floodplain. The Stone Oak corridor, in broker shorthand, refers to the larger 78258 zip code area — including newer additions north of Evans Road and east toward TPC Parkway. "North of 1604" Stone Oak, more colloquially, is sometimes used to describe everything from the Sonterra entry up through Bulverde-area new construction, even when those properties technically sit outside the original master-planned boundaries.

For the purposes of this guide, we use "Stone Oak" in the broader corridor sense — the residential geography between US 281 and the Cibolo Creek to the north and east, between Loop 1604 and the Comal County line to the south and north. This is the area most luxury buyers are evaluating when they say they are considering Stone Oak.

The Sub-Neighborhoods Within Stone Oak

One of the persistent misconceptions about Stone Oak is that it is a single homogeneous neighborhood. It is not. Stone Oak is a collection of distinct sub-communities, each with its own architectural era, HOA structure, price band, and buyer profile. Choosing the right sub-neighborhood matters as much as choosing Stone Oak itself.

Sonterra
$650K – $2.5M

Stone Oak's golf-anchored centerpiece, built around the Club at Sonterra's two championship courses. Mature oaks, country club access, and homes ranging from updated 1990s estates to recent custom builds.

Bentley Manor
$1.8M – $5M+

The ultra-private gated enclave at the heart of Stone Oak. Estates on half-acre to two-acre lots, custom European and contemporary architecture, and the most consistent ultra-luxury appreciation in the corridor.

The Heights of Stone Oak
$900K – $3M

Elevation-favored gated community known for hilltop views, generous setbacks, and a strong rotation of executive-level buyers. Excellent for families prioritizing both privacy and HOA-managed common areas.

Cibolo Canyons
$700K – $2.5M

Newer master-planned community north of the original Stone Oak, anchored by the JW Marriott TPC San Antonio resort and its two PGA-affiliated courses. Strong appeal to buyers wanting newer construction with resort access.

Inwood / Estates of Inwood
$700K – $1.6M

Established custom-home enclaves between Sonterra and Hardy Oak. Preferred by buyers wanting Stone Oak schools and feel without the ultra-premium pricing of Bentley Manor or The Heights.

Encino Park
$550K – $1.1M

The eastern Stone Oak corridor. Family-oriented, walkable, and still served by the same NEISD attendance zones that drive demand throughout Stone Oak. Strong choice for first-luxury buyers.

Beyond these, several smaller gated communities — Estancia, Trails of Vista Ridge, Fossil Ridge, and pockets along Hardy Oak — round out the corridor. We can map these in detail during a private consultation; the right sub-neighborhood almost always depends on what you are optimizing for: schools, commute, view, lot size, or HOA structure.

Schools and North East ISD

School quality is the single most consistent driver of Stone Oak demand. The corridor is served by North East Independent School District (NEISD), which is among the highest-performing large public districts in Greater San Antonio. The NEISD schools that serve Stone Oak attendance zones consistently earn A and B ratings from the Texas Education Agency, and the high schools — particularly Ronald Reagan High School and Claudia Taylor "Lady Bird" Johnson High School — rank among the top public high schools in the region for both academics and athletics.

The most commonly requested attendance zones in Stone Oak include:

Elementary: Hardy Oak Elementary, Bulverde Creek Elementary, Encino Park Elementary, Indian Springs Elementary, and Tuscany Heights Elementary. Each has a slightly different feeder pattern; we recommend buyers confirm specific street-level zoning before writing an offer, as the boundaries can shift across a single block.

Middle: Bush Middle School, Lopez Middle School, and Hill Country Middle School. Bush in particular has a long-standing reputation for advanced academics and music programs.

High: Reagan High School (north and central Stone Oak) and Johnson High School (eastern Stone Oak and Cibolo Canyons). Both feed the broader NEISD high school system and offer strong AP, dual-credit, and athletic programs.

For families considering private school, the corridor also has convenient access to TMI Episcopal, Saint Mary's Hall, and Antonian College Preparatory, all within a 15-to-25-minute drive. Proximity to private school options has become a secondary search criterion for relocating buyers in the past three years.

Daily Life: Parks, Dining, Retail

What separates Stone Oak from comparable luxury corridors in other Texas cities is how much of daily life can happen within a five-mile radius without ever needing to drive into the central city. Stone Oak Marketplace, The Shops at La Cantera (a short drive south on Loop 1604), and The Rim together provide an unusually deep retail and dining ecosystem — anchor tenants include Whole Foods, H-E-B Plus, Trader Joe's, Nordstrom, Apple, and a long list of regional and national restaurants.

For everyday recreation, Stone Oak Park offers more than 240 acres of preserved Hill Country terrain with hiking trails, bouldering, and seasonal wildflower fields — a genuine open-space asset rare for a neighborhood this central. Families also gravitate toward Hardberger Park (10 minutes south) and the JW Marriott TPC San Antonio resort for golf, spa, and pool day passes.

Healthcare is the other quiet luxury of the corridor. The North Central Baptist Hospital, Methodist Stone Oak, and the broader Methodist medical complex are all within Stone Oak itself, with the South Texas Medical Center a 15-to-20-minute drive via Loop 1604. For aging-in-place buyers and families with school-age children alike, this density of high-quality healthcare is a structural advantage.

"Stone Oak is the only San Antonio submarket where a buyer can get top-rated NEISD schools, walkable luxury retail, true open-space parks, and a 25-minute commute to downtown — all in the same zip code. That combination is what has held demand through every cycle."

The 2026 Luxury Market

The Stone Oak luxury market in 2025–26 has been characterized by three trends worth understanding before you make an offer.

1. Inventory has normalized but remains tight at the upper end. Across the broader Stone Oak corridor, active luxury inventory (homes priced above $700K) has rebuilt to a roughly 3.8-month supply — a balanced market by historical standards. Above $1.5M, however, supply remains lean, particularly in Bentley Manor and the gated communities of The Heights, where genuinely move-in-ready estates can attract competing offers within the first ten days on market.

2. Price-per-square-foot has held. Luxury sales between $700K and $1.5M traded at $215 to $260 per square foot in 2025, with the upper executive tier ($1.5M–$3M) clearing at $260 to $300 per square foot. Bentley Manor estates and select premium custom builds have transacted as high as $340 per square foot. These figures have held within a 4% band over the past 18 months — meaningful stability compared with the broader Texas luxury market.

3. Days on market are bifurcated. Well-priced, well-presented homes are clearing in 18 to 35 days. Aspirationally priced homes — particularly those still anchored to 2022 expectations — are sitting 90 to 160 days. The market is rewarding pricing discipline and high-quality presentation more than at any point in recent memory. For buyers, this means there is genuine negotiating leverage on listings approaching the 90-day mark.

The opportunity for serious buyers in 2026 is to work the market in two directions simultaneously: actively pursue the freshly listed inventory (where we can often introduce off-market and pre-market opportunities ahead of broader exposure), while keeping a calibrated watch on aged inventory where the negotiating environment is most favorable.

Who Stone Oak Is Right For

Stone Oak is right for buyers who want a luxury suburban environment with genuine community infrastructure — top schools, walkable retail, real parks, and a deep pool of comparable buyers and sellers that protects long-term resale. It is the strongest choice in San Antonio for executive families, dual-career professionals, and physicians and surgeons working out of the South Texas Medical Center or Methodist system.

Stone Oak is not the right choice for buyers prioritizing genuine acreage (head to Boerne, Cordillera, or the broader Hill Country for that), nor for buyers prioritizing in-town historic character (Alamo Heights, Olmos Park, or Terrell Hills are better aligned). It is also not the choice for buyers who want resort or vacation-property ambiance (consider New Braunfels waterfront or the Fredericksburg corridor instead).

For everyone in between — the buyer who wants the best of San Antonio's residential infrastructure on a half-acre lot in a gated community, with schools that are genuinely a non-issue and a commute that is genuinely manageable — Stone Oak is, in our view, the strongest single-decision answer in the city.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where exactly is Stone Oak in San Antonio?

Stone Oak is a master-planned community in far north central San Antonio, generally bounded by US 281 to the west, Loop 1604 to the south, and the Cibolo Creek corridor to the north and east. It sits roughly 17 miles north of downtown San Antonio, in the 78258 zip code.

Is Stone Oak a good place to live in 2026?

Yes — Stone Oak consistently ranks among San Antonio's top family-luxury communities. The combination of A-rated NEISD schools, walkable retail at Stone Oak Marketplace and The Rim, real open space at Stone Oak Park, and a deep luxury home inventory between $700K and $4M+ makes it the strongest single-decision answer for executive families in the city.

What school district serves Stone Oak?

Stone Oak is served by North East Independent School District (NEISD). Highly-rated NEISD schools serving the area include Hardy Oak, Bulverde Creek, Encino Park, Indian Springs, and Tuscany Heights elementaries; Bush, Lopez, and Hill Country middle schools; and Reagan and Johnson high schools. Always confirm street-level zoning before writing an offer.

What is the average price of a luxury home in Stone Oak?

Luxury homes in Stone Oak typically begin near $700,000 and extend through $4M+ for estate properties in Bentley Manor, The Heights of Stone Oak, Sonterra, and Cibolo Canyons. The bulk of luxury sales in 2025–26 have traded between $215 and $300 per square foot, with select premium builds reaching $340/sqft.

How long is the commute from Stone Oak to downtown San Antonio?

The commute from Stone Oak to downtown San Antonio is typically 25–35 minutes outside of rush hour via US 281 South. Peak morning traffic can extend that to 40–50 minutes. The South Texas Medical Center is 15–20 minutes via Loop 1604. The San Antonio International Airport is 12–18 minutes south via US 281.

Are there gated communities in Stone Oak?

Yes — Bentley Manor, The Heights of Stone Oak, Estancia, and several smaller enclaves are fully gated. Sonterra and Cibolo Canyons offer a mix of gated and non-gated sections. We can match the right gated community to your privacy, lot-size, and HOA preferences during a private consultation.

What is the property tax rate in Stone Oak?

Stone Oak sits within Bexar County, with combined property tax rates that vary slightly by sub-neighborhood and overlapping districts. Most Stone Oak addresses fall in the 2.0%–2.4% range of assessed value annually. We provide a sub-neighborhood-specific tax estimate as part of every offer analysis.
